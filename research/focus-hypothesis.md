# Focus Hypothesis

## Where We Are

After two research sprints and an initial segmentation exercise, one group continues to stand out.

For now, I'm calling them **Regional Hub Builders**.

These are aspiring developers learning and growing within emerging tech ecosystems that receive far less attention than major technology hubs.

Many are learning with limited local mentorship, fewer opportunities to collaborate in person, and additional infrastructure challenges.

At the same time, they're often deeply motivated—not just to grow personally, but to strengthen the communities they're belong to.

## Why This Group?

Based on the research so far, this group appears to sit at the intersection of several challenges:

- navigating the transition from learning to contributing
- limited access to experienced mentors
- fewer locally relevant opportunities
- additional environmental constraints

These observations make this group an interesting place to continue researching.

## Important

This is not a conclusion.

It's simply the strongest hypothesis we have today.

The next phase of OpenDoor is to test whether this hypothesis reflects the experiences of real people.
