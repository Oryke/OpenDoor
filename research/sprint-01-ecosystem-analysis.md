# Sprint 01 — Ecosystem Analysis

## Objective

To understand the current ecosystem supporting beginner developers and identify opportunities where OpenDoor could provide meaningful value.

---

## What We Researched

This sprint focused on exploring existing beginner-focused communities, learning platforms, mentorship initiatives, open-source programs, and educational resources.

The goal was to understand what already exists before deciding whether OpenDoor should become a product, community, or something entirely different.

---

## Key Findings

### Existing support for beginners is strong

There are many communities, platforms, and organizations dedicated to helping people learn programming and make their first open-source contribution.

### The ecosystem is fragmented

Most initiatives focus on one part of the beginner journey rather than supporting learners throughout the entire experience.

### A possible gap exists

One recurring observation is the period between completing tutorials and confidently contributing to real projects.

For now, this is being referred to as **"the lonely middle."**

This is a working hypothesis, not a conclusion, and will require further validation through research and interviews.

---

## Assumptions Challenged

Before this research, the assumption was that beginners mainly needed a friendlier community.

The research suggests the problem may be broader than community alone and could involve confidence, direction, motivation, or transitioning from learning into practice.

---

## Questions Raised

- Is the "lonely middle" experienced by all beginners or only certain groups?
- What causes learners to stop during this stage?
- Which existing initiatives already address this problem?
- What evidence would validate or disprove this hypothesis?

---

## Next Sprint

Research Sprint 02 will focus on validating the problem itself using published research, reports, and evidence before moving on to interviews.
