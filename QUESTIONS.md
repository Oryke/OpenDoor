# Questions We're Exploring

OpenDoor is still in its research phase. These are the questions guiding our work.

---

## Understanding the Problem

- Why do beginners stop learning technology?
- At what stage do most beginners lose confidence?
- Is the biggest challenge technical, emotional, or social?
- What makes someone feel like they don't belong in tech?

---

## Community

- What makes a beginner-friendly community truly beginner-friendly?
- How do experienced developers create welcoming environments?
- What behaviours unintentionally discourage newcomers?
- What does psychological safety look like in online communities?

---

## Learning

- What does a great first week of learning look like?
- How much information is too much for a beginner?
- What kinds of projects build confidence early?
- Should learning be self-paced, guided, or community-driven?

---

## Open Source

- Why are beginners afraid to contribute?
- What makes a "good first issue" actually beginner-friendly?
- How can first contributions feel rewarding instead of stressful?
- How can maintainers better support new contributors?

---

## Opportunities

- What already exists?
- Where are existing communities falling short?
- What unique value could OpenDoor provide?
- What should we intentionally avoid becoming?

---

## Questions We Still Need to Answer

This list will continue to evolve as we learn more through research, conversations, and experimentation.
