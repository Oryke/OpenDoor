# OpenDoor Interview Guide

## Why I'm doing these interviews

So far, OpenDoor has been shaped by published research, existing communities, and observations from the wider developer ecosystem.

That's a good place to start, but it's not enough.

The next step is to hear directly from people who are living this journey.

I'm not conducting these interviews to prove OpenDoor is needed or to validate an idea I've already decided on. I'm doing them because I want to understand how people actually experience learning to code, getting stuck, asking for help, and taking their first steps toward contributing to real projects.

If these conversations completely change the direction of OpenDoor, that's a good outcome.

The goal is to learn—not to be right.

---

# A few reminders before every interview

- Ask about things that actually happened, not what someone thinks they might do.
- Stay curious.
- Don't introduce OpenDoor or explain the idea until the interview is over.
- Let people tell their story in their own words.
- Don't rush to reassure, advise, or solve their problems.
- Allow silence. People often say the most valuable things after they've had a moment to think.
- Follow interesting stories instead of rigidly sticking to the questions.

The purpose of these interviews is to understand people better, not to confirm existing assumptions.

---

# Phase 1 — Their Story

## 1. Tell me the story of how you started learning to code.

**Question**

> What got you into it, and what has the journey looked like so far?

**What I'm listening for**

Their motivation, background, and how they naturally describe their journey without being led.

---

## 2. What does a typical week of learning or coding look like for you right now?

**What I'm listening for**

Daily realities, constraints, available time, access to equipment, internet reliability, and who they learn alongside.

---

## 3. Can you tell me about a time recently when you felt like you were making real progress?

**Question**

> What was going on?

**What I'm listening for**

How they personally define progress and success.

---

# Phase 2 — Beyond Tutorials

## 4. Have you ever looked into contributing to a project that wasn't your own?

**Question**

> Tell me about that.

**What I'm listening for**

Whether they've even reached this stage, and if not, what has kept them from it.

---

## 5. If you have looked for something to work on, walk me through what that actually looked like, step by step.

**What I'm listening for**

How they searched, chose, or abandoned projects.

---

## 6. Have you ever tried to get someone else's project running on your computer?

**Question**

> Tell me about the last time.

**What I'm listening for**

Technical onboarding, setup frustrations, and how they reacted when things became difficult.

---

# Phase 3 — Getting Stuck

## 7. When you don't understand something, what do you usually do next?

**What I'm listening for**

Their default behaviour when they encounter uncertainty.

---

## 8. Tell me about a specific time you asked someone for help with something code-related.

**Question**

> What made you decide to ask, and how did it go?

**What I'm listening for**

How comfortable they are seeking help and what makes them feel supported.

---

## 9. Has there been a time you wanted to ask a question publicly—in a forum, Discord, GitHub, or anywhere else—but decided not to?

**Question**

> What was going through your mind?

**What I'm listening for**

Invisible moments where hesitation prevented action.

---

# Phase 4 — Sharing Their Work

## 10. Tell me about a time you shared something you made with someone else for feedback.

**Question**

> What was that experience like?

**What I'm listening for**

How they experience feedback and evaluation.

---

## 11. If you've ever submitted something and then had to wait to hear back—an assignment, an application, a pull request, anything—

**Question**

> What was that waiting period like?

**What I'm listening for**

How uncertainty and silence affect their motivation.

---

## 12. Tell me about a time you got stuck on something technical and eventually gave up on it, even if only for a while.

**Question**

> What led to that?

**What I'm listening for**

How people quietly disengage and what ultimately causes them to stop.

---

# Phase 5 — Looking Back

## 13. Who, if anyone, do you turn to when you're stuck?

**Question**

> How did that become your go-to person or place?

**What I'm listening for**

The strength—or absence—of their support network.

---

## 14. Tell me about someone you know personally who you'd describe as "a real developer."

**Question**

> What, in your mind, makes them different from you?

**What I'm listening for**

Their beliefs about belonging, confidence, and identity.

---

## 15. Looking back over everything we've talked about, what's one moment that stands out the most from your journey so far?

**What I'm listening for**

The experience they believe mattered most, rather than the one I expected to matter.
